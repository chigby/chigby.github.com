You Make The Card: Shackles of Syphoning

Originally posted April 17th, 2013

In 2013, Wizards of the Coast held an online event where people were allowed to design, submit, and vote on ideas for a Magic card. Herein, I ruminated on what that might look like. Over ten years later, it’s cute how quaint this looks.

I ended up spending more time than I thought I would on this. Shackles of Syphoning was the second design I came up with, but I kept going until I had templated a moderner version of Purgatory, a bizarre mash-up of Demonic Consultation, Liar’s Pendulum, and Choice of Damnations, which would have been better off as a sorcery and probably had too much text anyway, Smokestack-in-reverse, and a possibly fun, slightly asymmetrical but sort of unexciting Deathtouch monger thing.

There may still be latent potential in those ideas. I might revist them. Yet the more I thought about the Shackles of Syphoning idea, the more I liked it. From a flavor perspective, I imagine this as enchanted bonds which immobilizes or weakens all of your creatures and ties them together into a dark, mana-woven web that leeches life from your enemies. You are putting them on a magical leash, giving them only enough freedom to defend you, and sapping any excess energy from them to fuel your life-syphoning spell. Seems like something a black mage would do.
